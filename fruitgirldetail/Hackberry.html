It was hidden in the Capilano Valley, a stretch of the Capilano River in North Vancouver. The 137-meter-long Capilano Suspension Bridge, the world's longest suspension bridge for walking, is said to be Vancouver's oldest tourist attraction, where visitors can enjoy forest bathing and spectacular views.
